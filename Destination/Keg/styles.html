The Compact Appliance colors should be predominatly neutral to allow feature content to be the center focus. Primary Call to Actions (CTA) to take center stage and grab attention. The brand colors should be used sparingly with ratio of mainly base and system colors.

Brand Colors & Theme

These are the primary theme colors that make up the CompactAppliance.com web color pallet. There are additional colors available that are used to add depth and enrich this experience. With complement, system and base colors you can extend the CompactAppliance.com brand.

$color-base-a
#ffffff

Base Palette
The background color for the site and almost all page content.

$color-brand-b
#cfedf3

Brand Palette
The light background color for any important content related to the brand.

$color-brand-e
#267d92

Brand Palette
The dark background color for any important content related to the brand.

$color-base-b
#f3f3f3

Base Palette
The light background color for any other content/element where the base palette won't work.


Brand colors and base colors compliment each other. Even though everything on the site can be considered part of the brand we do not want to make everything the brand color - that would be too much of one color and delute the brand colors' effectiveness to call attention. Therefore, we use brand colors for visual emphasis, not as our primary go-to color. We want the brand colors to be used strategically and for important elements or areas of a page that are tied to our logo, brand purpose, or content that differentiates us as a brand from our competitors.

Color Balance

90% of the color used on any page of the site come from the Background & Border colors but there are also a few select colors that are used for emphasis and grabbing the user's attention. The chart to the right illustratues the proportion of colors you should try to adhere to when designing.

If you are having difficulty balancing the use of color on a page please consult with a designer. It also helps to prioritize the content and CTAs on a page by what is most important in terms of grabbing the user's attention. Then only emphasize the important element(s) on a page and downplay the rest accordingly.

Color Proportions

Try to shoot for these percentages of color for every page.

  • White 52.5
  • Ash 15
  • Ice 7.5
  • Teal 17.5
  • Dark Canary 2.5
  • Dark Leaf 2.5
  • Dark Coral 2.5
